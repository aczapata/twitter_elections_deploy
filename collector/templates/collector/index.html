    What is Twitter Elections?

    Twitter Elections is the implementation of Sentiment Analysis Tool in the 2016 United States Elections. We use tweets in order to determine the opinions of Twitter Users towards the political candidates and parties during the pre-election period.

    What is being analyzed?

    We have taked samples of tweets during important events of the pre-election period (Super Tuesday, Florida Primaries, Republican Debate, Democratic Debate) using the Twitter API. We have chosen tweets that contain the words: Donald Trump, Hillary Clinton, Marco Rubio, Ted Cruz, Bernie Sanders and the hashtags: #USElections, #SuperTuesday, etc.

    How does it works?

    We apply different methods to determine the Sentiment of a tweet in order to determine if it is positive, neutral or negative. To do so we have implemented two Lexicons: Sentlex and SO-CAL and a Machine Learning techinique: Naive Bayes Classifier.
